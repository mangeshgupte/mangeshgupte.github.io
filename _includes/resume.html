Publications

News Posting by Strategic Users in a Social Network

Workshop on Internet and Network Economics
Mangesh Gupte, M.T. Hajiaghayi, Lu Han, Liviu Iftode, Pravin Shankar, Raluca Ursu.
Presented at WINE '09 in Rome, Italy.

We study the spread of news in a Social Network when the users are strategic and show that news propagation follows a threshold phenomenon, hence, high quality information provably spreads throughout the network while low quality news spreads to very few nodes in the network. We simulate these strategies on a sample of the Twitter social graph to confirm the results on real networks.

Universally Optimal Privacy Mechanisms for Minimax Agents

Symposium on Principles of Database Systems
Mangesh Gupte, Mukund Sundararajan
Presented at PODS '10 in Indianapolis, IN

We design a differentially private mechanism to release the result of any fixed count query, such that it is simultaneously optimal for all minimax information consumers. Additionally, we show how to release query results at multiple levels of privacy in a collusion-resistant manner.

Finding hierarchy in directed online social networks

Conference on World Wide Web
Mangesh Gupte, Pravin Shankar, Jing Li, S. Muthukrishnan, Liviu Iftode
Presented at WWW '11 in Hyderabad, India.

We study social hierarchy and social stratification using online social networks and define a new measure of hierarchy in directed social networks. We give a polynomial time algorithm to compute this measure. We apply it to Twitter, Flickr, delicious and YouTube and show how hierarchy emerges as the network grows. The measure gives us an alternate way to use links in directed social networks to rank nodes in the network.

Measuring Tie Strength in Implicit Social Networks

ACM Conference on Web Science (WebSci '12)
Mangesh Gupte, Tina Eliassi-Rad

We address the problem of measuring connectedness or tie strength between each pair of persons given the set of events they attend. Starting from a list of axioms, which a measure of tie strength must satisfy, we characterize functions that satisfy all the axioms. A measure of tie strength induces a ranking on the edges of the social network. We show that for applications where the ranking, and not the absolute value of the tie strength, is the important thing about the measure, the axioms are equivalent to a natural partial order. In our experiments, we demonstrate the efficacy of our approach; show the completeness and soundness of our axioms, and present Kendall Tau Rank Correlation between various tie-strength measures.

Analysis of Cardinal Auctions

Algorithmica, September ’13
Mangesh Gupte, Darja Krushevskaja, S Muthukrishnan

We study cardinal auctions for selling multiple copies of a good, in which bidders specify not only their bid or how much they are ready to pay for the good, but also a cardinality constraint on the number of copies that will be sold via the auction. We perform first known Price of Anarchy type analyses with detailed comparison of the classical Vickrey-Clarke-Groves (VCG) auction and one based on minimum pay property (MPP) which is similar to Generalized Second Price auction commonly used in sponsored search.
